Bradly Alicea has a MA in Anthropology/Zoology from the University of Florida and a PhD in Cognitive Science and Computational Biology from Michigan State University. He has published in multiple academic fields, and in venues such as Frontiers in Cellular Neuroscience, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, Stem Cells and Development, Biosystems, and Proceedings of Artificial Life. With interests spanning the biological, computational, and social sciences, he is currently Head Scientist and Founder of Orthogonal Research, Open-source Community Manager at the Rokwire initiative, a Senior Contributor at the OpenWorm Foundation, and administrator of Synthetic Daisies blog.
